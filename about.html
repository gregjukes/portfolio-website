About

My Story

I started my career as a performing artist, creating shows in which instrumental musicians are also actors and dancers. When I decided to develop a new skillset outside the performing arts, user experience design was a natural choice because the principles of human centered design that drive the field are so similar to my process crafting a show, thinking about the emotional journey I offer audiences.

My conservatory training is as a classical music percussionist and one of my favorite things about my instrument is that it’s made for collaboration. Percussion encompasses anything you strike or shake to make a sound from maracas to marimbas, drums, cymbals, bells, hundreds of instruments from all over the world. As a percussionist, you have to be conversant in many styles of music and you need to be highly adaptable whether that means responding to the musicians you’re playing with on stage or figuring out how to play a different instrument with each or your limbs. This meshes with my passion for UX design in that both fields are about solving interesting problems to create great experiences for people!

I am based in Somerville, Massachusetts which is just north of Boston in the USA. My performing work has taken me all over North America and I have been doing remote work internationally for years with a studio of online drum students in Saudi Arabia.
